HobbyCell

@2022/01/16

Startup idea for 3D printing friendly electronic components which can be picked & placed by the 3D printer itself. Moreover, it works as a Lego for people to play with.

Similar to this:

Except targeted at higher level blocks, like Bluetooth and Wi-Fi and CPU cells.

Cells are relatively cheap, so you can make something like an alarm clock for ~5-10‚Ç¨.

Cells may have magnets inside, so they snap to each other, or they snap together like lego blocks.

Cells are made to be compatible with each other (voltage, communication and other stuff), and the emphasis is placed on programming, being open and the ability to communicate with each other.

The idea is that the user can actually make their own applications, and not fiddle with stuff like transistors or whatever. It‚Äôs not just an education toy, but a ready-made universal block to be used in your applications, no matter if you are a hobbyist, or a small-scale business making low production batches of 3D-printed items.

Do you want to make some 3D-printed item that contains electronics? You don‚Äôt need tools, wires, and soldering station. Just stack a bunch of cells inside, and you are done. No need to fiddle with wires. Everything is maximally user-friendly, works together and is ready for small-scale automation. If you need, you can use pick & place extension for 3D printer and insert them automatically during the print.

This is not a toy, it‚Äôs a tool that abstracts away the wires, so you can just snap things together and be done with the hardware.

Cell design

10√ó10x5 mm cells with magnets inside. Cells snap together when placed next to each other.

Each cell has connection points on the sides, but unlike traditional electronics packages, also on top / bottom. They can be stacked on top of each other, or next to each other.

Each cell shares power with other cells using the connection points.

They also share internal data pins, as well as other power pins.

Pins:

  • Two for power
  • Two for other power (can be used by solar cells or whatever)
  • 1-4 for communication

Some cells are specialized, like surface cells for solar power or displays or buttons. They can still be stacked on top of other cells.

This means that you can stack them in 2D as a big surface, or in 3D in any way you like / need.

Different kinds of cells

  1. Compute cell
  1. Battery cell

  1. LED cell
  1. Button cell
  1. Display cell
  1. Solar cell.
  1. IR cell
  1. Wireless cell
    1. Extremely low-power communication with neighboring cells wirelessly, over the distance of 10-50 cm.
  1. Thermometer cell
  1. Microphone cell
  1. Speaker cell
  1. Power delivery cells
    1. With external pins to connect external power to.

Moreover, wide possibilities for all kinds of specialized cells:

  1. Stacked cells
    1. Big radio cells
    1. Big battery cells
    1. Full color display cell
    1. Bluetooth cell
    1. Wi-Fi cell
    1. Computer cells
    1. Relay cells
  1. Motor driver cells
  1. Radio cell
  1. Spacer cell
    1. Does nothing, just routes power next
  1. Separator cell
    1. Separates ‚Äúother power‚Äù wires from the rest of the circuit.
    1. So, for example, you can place battery cell, solar cell and charger cells next to each other, then separate them with a separator cell, and then you can use other power circuits to drive a relay or whatever.
  1. LED grid cell
  1. Gyro cells

And so on ..

Software

The user just slaps some cells together and then programs them, using IDE & programmer cell.

User doesn‚Äôt worry about communication. This is all abstracted. If I want to communicate with a display, I just use:

display = async_endpoint(type.display)
display.send(data)

If there are multiple display cells connected, I can simply address them by the serial number, which is laser etched on the cell from the bottom. But I can also use interactive query to get all cells connected to the bus.

Everything should be automate-friendly. This means that if the cell is used in automated 3D printers with pick&place machine, the machine can query the cell‚Äôs ID, or maybe automatically read it from the QR code burned on the surface of the cell.

Communication

By default, cells connected physically together communicate via some kind of wired protocol, and provide power for the whole bus.

It should be definitely possible to communicate with other cells via some kind of wireless protocol. For example, I can make one cell stack for the weather station and put it on my balcony, and communication with my alarm clock should be effortless.

Moreover, communication with computers, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and all kinds of other devices should be effortless too. I can easily pipe data from my weather station to my desktop computer.

ESP chips with both Wi-Fi and Bluetooth are super cheap these days.

Economy

Basic cells should cost as little as possible, and all of them fixed price.

For example: Compute cell, battery cell, solar cell each cost 2‚Ç¨, even though they may cost more to build. The money is recouped on stupid cells, like button cell or spacer cells.

Reasoning: User will typically need one or two compute cells, but much more periphery cells.

More specific cells like heavy compute or solar cell may cost more.

Ecology

When the product is not needed anymore, it can be deconstructed back to the cells, which are universal and can always be reused in some other project.

This makes sense for people who make products using 3D printing.

Bootstrapping

At the beginning, maybe make bigger 3D-printed cells, on the resin printer, and fill them with electronics manually.

Or perhaps do a form for resin and ‚Äúmass produce‚Äù them in small batches, of let say 50 by filling the form with resin?

The key to success would be mass production, where the things are made from one piece, except for maybe batteries, which should be removable from the cover.

Cells could be made by the same process as chip packages, one blob of dark plastic matter, with just connection points sticking out.

Maybe figure how to make everything from some flexible PCB and just fold it so that it covers all six sides and provides connectors? Idk.
